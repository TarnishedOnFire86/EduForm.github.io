How various disabilities affect students

- Dyslexia

Dyslexia affects reading and spelling, causing trouble decoding words or recognizing sounds. Students struggle with reading fluency and comprehension, needing extra time or structured literacy programs to succeed in class. (International Dyslexia Association)

- Dysgraphia

Dysgraphia impairs writing, leading to poor handwriting and difficulty expressing thoughts. Students face challenges with note-taking and written tasks, often requiring keyboards or extra time to complete assignments. (National Center for Learning Disabilities)

- Dyscalculia

Dyscalculia hinders understanding numbers and math concepts. Students find it tough to memorize tables or solve problems, impacting math classes. They benefit from visual aids or calculators to support learning. (LDRFA)

- ADHD

ADHD disrupts focus and impulse control, making it hard for students to stay on task or follow instructions. This affects class participation and homework completion, often requiring structured routines or breaks. (CDC)
